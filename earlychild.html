Measuring child skills

A simple model of child development

The dynamics of child development has been increasingly a subject of study among researches in a wide range of disciplines. A simple theoretical framework is based on the idea of fixed, discrete set of skills (e.g. cognition, socioemotional, prysical health, nutrition) which evolve over time based on an unknown accumulation process (i.e. techonology). Similarly, we can describe the parental decisions regarging child invesments as a function of child characteristics, household constrains and prices (e.g. alternative cost of time). In both cases we need to allow for shocks, i.e. unexpected events that can alter parental decisions but also the capacity of children to accumulate skills (e.g. natural disasters). Ultimately, in onder to characterize, describe and predict skills, we are interested in two key steps. First, is important to measure different skills and parental investments through simple but information-rich indexes, in order to avoid dealing with too many moving pieces. Secondly, given a set of skills available, we would like to describe the evolution of skills across individuals and communities, in order to inform stakeholders as well as target, monitor and evaluate programs.
